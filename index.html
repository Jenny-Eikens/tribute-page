Harriet Tubman

Abolitionist, suffragist and civil rights activist

Here's an overview of some of Harriet Tubman's most important life events:

  • ca. 1822 - born into slavery as Araminta Ross in Dorchester County, Maryland
  • 1844 - marries free black man John Tubman, adopts her mother's first name Harriet soon after
  • 1849 - escapes to Philadelphia
  • 1849 - becomes an operator of the Underground Railroad (a secret network providing shelter and assistance to escaping slaves)
  • 1849-1859 - returns to Maryland at least 13 times to rescue enslaved people (including her family), earning her the nickname 'Moses'
  • 1850 - the Fugitive Slave Act is passed, Tubman helps guide escapees farther north into British North America (Canada)
  • 1858 - meets prominent abolitionist John Brown and helps him prepare his raid on the town of Harpers Ferry, West Virginia
  • 1861 - the Civil War begins, Tubman works for the Union Army
  • 1863 - Tubman guides the raid on Combahee Ferry (which liberated more than 700 enslaved people), making her the first woman to lead an armed military operation in the US
  • 1865 - the war ends, Tubman retires to her family home in Auburn, New York
  • 1913 - Tubman dies in a nursing home for African Americans she helped establish
"There was one of two things I had a right to: liberty or death. If I could not have one, I would take the other, for no man should take me alive. I should fight for liberty as long as my strength lasted." - Harriet Tubman
